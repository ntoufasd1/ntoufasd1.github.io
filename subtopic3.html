Misinformation, Bias, and Digital Literacy

CSIT 100 • Webography • Dina Ntoufas • 24 November 2025

Source A
URLWestern Michigan University
Site authorTeaching & Learning Office
AbstractThis article warns that students may accept AI-generated content without questioning its accuracy. It highlights the importance of digital literacy and critical evaluation skills to detect bias and misinformation. The resource encourages educators to teach students how to assess the credibility of AI responses.
Source B
URLBMC Psychology
Site authorZhang, Tian, & Lu
AbstractThe study discusses how AI-generated content may reflect biases from training data. It stresses the need for students to develop judgment skills to evaluate AI output critically. The authors recommend integrating digital literacy into AI-supported learning environments to prevent passive acceptance of flawed information.
